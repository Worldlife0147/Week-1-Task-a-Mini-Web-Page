My Favorite Animal

The okapi, often called the forest giraffe, is a captivating animal native to the rainforests of the Democratic Republic of the Congo, which borders Nigeria and shares similar West African ecosystems. I love the okapi for its unique zebra-like stripes on its legs combined with a giraffe-like body, making it a living puzzle of nature that highlights the biodiversity of African forests.

  • Okapis have an extraordinarily long, prehensile tongue—up to 18 inches long—that they use to strip leaves from branches and even clean their own eyes and ears.
  • Despite their striking appearance, okapis are masters of camouflage in the dense rainforest, blending seamlessly with sunlight patterns through their striped legs.
  • They are the giraffe's closest living relative, but unlike their taller cousins, okapis thrive in the understory of rainforests and were unknown to Western science until 1901.
